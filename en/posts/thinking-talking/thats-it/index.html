Editor's Note for 'The Emerald Green Eye'

八月 5, 2025

Sometimes it’s hard for people to break free from the arrogance and prejudice branded into them by their era. I can only express myself through music with all my might.

Unicode characters were born very early, but it wasn’t until the era of large language models that human electronic knowledge finally approached unification.

Characters, words, grammar, semantics—countless symbols flow into the torrent of human history through self-description, allowing countless people to be forced to choose what they want to become.

What do I want to express? What can I express?

I can’t figure it out.

Music must be deconstructible, that’s what I think.

So I wrote “One-50000th”, “The Long Goodbye”, “The Place Never Reached”, “Black Cat Appears in Daylight”, and the last one, as the final piece, is responsible for deconstructing everything about the music I’ve created.

However, can this really be called creation? Do I really have my own thoughts? Am I really better at thinking than large language models?

Thinking is really such a thankless task, just like my nearly 30 years of life—ordinary, common, just like my profile full of idle chatter.

The idle chatter of ordinary people.

“You know what? One in every 25 people has antisocial personality disorder, but being antisocial is neither a derogatory term nor a complimentary one.” I heard Him say that.

Yes, that’s right, but, I said, how should you define society?
